<h2 id="1">Wrap text in <span style="font-size: 30.9985px;">Microsoft</span> Excel 2013:</h2>
<h3>Method 1:</h3>
<ul>
 	<li><strong>Select</strong> the cell that you want to<strong> use the Wrap text option.</strong></li>
 	<li><strong style="font-size: 19px;">Go to the menu bar → click the Home menu → Alignment option, </strong>there you can find the<strong style="font-size: 19px;"> Wrap Text.</strong></li>
</ul>
[caption id="attachment_5895" align="aligncenter" width="1025"]<img class="wp-image-5895 size-full" src="https://geekexcel.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/Screenshot_1-22.png" alt="Menu" width="1025" height="497" /> Menu[/caption]
<ul>
 	<li>Once you click the <strong>wrap text option</strong> then the selected cell gets<strong> wrapped.</strong></li>
</ul>
[caption id="attachment_5896" align="aligncenter" width="930"]<img class="wp-image-5896 size-full" src="https://geekexcel.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/Screenshot_2-22.png" alt="Wrapped text" width="930" height="612" /> Wrapped text[/caption]
<h3>Method 2:</h3>
<ul>
 	<li><strong>Right-click</strong> on the cell, the<strong> popup menu</strong> gets opened in that, select the<strong> format cells option</strong>.</li>
</ul>
[caption id="attachment_5897" align="aligncenter" width="1113"]<img class="wp-image-5897 size-full" src="https://geekexcel.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/Screenshot_3-21.png" alt="Popup menu" width="1113" height="755" /> Popup menu[/caption]
<ul>
 	<li>In the <strong>Format cells window, </strong>select the <strong>Alignment tab</strong> and <strong>check in</strong> the <strong>Wrap</strong> <strong>text</strong> in the <strong>Text control option.</strong></li>
</ul>
[caption id="attachment_5898" align="aligncenter" width="1136"]<img class="wp-image-5898 size-full" src="https://geekexcel.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/Screenshot_4-22.png" alt="Format cells window" width="1136" height="661" /> Format cells window[/caption]
<ul>
 	<li>The cell gets <strong>wrapped</strong> in your Excel sheet.</li>
</ul>
